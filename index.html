My First Exposure to Coding

In April of 2016, I participated in the first hackathon held at IUPUI called JagHack. During this 24 hour period, my team members and I worked on developing an intelligent Android based alarm application that focused on the optimization of sleep through tracking sleep cycles. My roles with this project were involved in assisting with graphic design with the logo, developing the business pitch, and most importantly working on the user-interface design features for the application. Under the mentorship of a user interface design consultant, I was able to map out essential features for the app concerning the selection of sleep cycles on the basis of factors such as desired time and desired number of sleep cycles. However, I lacked the technical capabilities and programming knowledge to integrate these features into the mobile application that my other team members possessed. Health and wellness technology is also a burgeoning interest of mine and I think having a background in a programming language such as JavaScript could greatly help me develop and apply the user experience based ideas I have for such future health and wellness based applications. I also hope to better understand such processes that were required to develop the mobile application, like requesting an API for creating a geolocation feature.
